# If I Wanted to Make My First $100K in 2026, I’d Do This

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jfW6gL6hKhk

## Key Ideas
- Reduce expenses to increase available cash flow for learning and growth
- Use free time (before/after work) to build skills instead of consuming content
- Focus on one skill that people already pay for
- Use a structured daily system: promotion, delivery, and building
- Learning requires action and feedback, not just consuming information
- Reinvest money into tools, skills, and experiments
- Avoid lifestyle inflation to build real savings

## Summary
This video explains a step-by-step approach to reaching the first $100K by focusing on saving money, managing time effectively, learning high-value skills, and reinvesting resources into growth.

## Transcript (Partial)
When starting out, it’s important to reduce living expenses as much as possible. This creates extra cash flow that can be reinvested into learning and skill development.

Time is equally important. Instead of wasting free time, it should be used to build skills and create opportunities. A structured approach to time includes promotion, delivery, and building.

Learning is defined as behavior change. Simply consuming content is not enough—real learning happens through repetition, feedback, and improvement.

Focusing on one skill, one audience, and one channel is key to building momentum. Trying to do too many things at once slows progress.

Money should be reinvested into tools, mentorship, and experimentation. Growth comes from taking action and learning from mistakes.

Finally, avoiding lifestyle inflation is critical. Increasing income without controlling spending prevents real wealth accumulation.

## Initial Insights
- Content (including YouTube) can be used as a tool for promotion and demand generation
- Skill acquisition is the foundation of business growth, not shortcuts or hacks
- Consistency and repetition are more important than perfection
- Business growth is a combination of time management, focus, and continuous learning
